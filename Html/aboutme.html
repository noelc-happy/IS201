About Me

Header

I've painted since I was around 6 years old and always dreamed to do paintings for my church!

I paint because it allows me to express feelings and thoughts that words often fail to capture. There’s something deeply grounding and healing about putting brush to canvas—watching colors blend, forms take shape, and emotions find a voice. Painting helps me slow down, reflect, and connect more deeply with the beauty of the world around me. It’s a space where I feel free to be creative, honest, and intentional. Each piece I create holds a part of me—my faith, my memories, and my hope to uplift others through something visual and lasting.

Painting has always been a part of me—something I’ve loved since I was a child. Growing up surrounded by nature and open spaces, I found endless inspiration in the world around me: the way light moves through trees, the colors of the sky at sunset, or the quiet stillness of early mornings. Art became my way of capturing those moments and holding onto them. Over time, it’s become more than just a hobby; it’s a form of worship, a way to honor the beauty God has created and share it with others in a personal, meaningful way.

I also paint because I want to make an impact. In a world that often feels rushed, divided, or distracted, I believe art can remind people to slow down and feel again—to notice, to wonder, and to heal. Whether I’m painting a landscape, a person, or something abstract, my hope is always the same: that someone might look at it and feel seen, inspired, or comforted. Painting allows me to connect with people without needing to say a word, and that quiet power is what keeps me coming back to the canvas again and again.
